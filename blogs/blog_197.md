## FAQ Section

**What is the critical difference between Replacement Cost and Actual Cash Value?**
This is the most important distinction in commercial property insurance. Replacement Cost coverage pays out the exact amount required to repair or rebuild your damaged property using new materials at today’s current market prices, without any deduction for depreciation. Actual Cash Value, however, aggressively deducts for age and wear-and-tear before paying out. If a ten-year-old roof is destroyed, an Actual Cash Value policy will only pay a fraction of what a new roof actually costs, leaving the business owner to pay massive out-of-pocket expenses.

**Why is business interruption coverage just as important as physical property coverage?**
While physical property coverage pays to rebuild your damaged facility, business interruption coverage is what actually keeps your company alive during the massive downtime. If a severe fire shuts down your operations for eight months, you still have to pay rent on alternate spaces, service heavy bank loans, and pay your key employees. Business interruption insurance replaces your lost net income and covers these critical ongoing expenses, ensuring the company does not go completely bankrupt while waiting for construction to finish.

**What is Ordinance or Law coverage, and why is it absolutely essential?**
Ordinance or Law coverage is a critical endorsement that covers the massive, unexpected costs of complying with new, stricter building codes. If your older building is severely damaged, local authorities will not allow you to rebuild it exactly as it was; they will legally mandate that you upgrade the electrical systems, add modern fire sprinklers, or improve ADA accessibility. Standard commercial property policies explicitly exclude these massive upgrade costs, meaning without this specific endorsement, you will be forced to pay for those mandatory upgrades entirely out of pocket.

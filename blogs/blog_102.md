## FAQ

**When should a startup hire a financial advisor?**
A startup should engage a specialized financial advisor as soon as they secure their first significant round of institutional funding. Early engagement ensures that founder equity is structured tax-efficiently and that corporate cash management strategies are put in place to extend runway safely.

**What is the difference between an accountant and a financial advisor for startups?**
An accountant focuses on historical data, tax compliance, and bookkeeping. A financial advisor for startups is forward-looking; they handle strategic cash flow modeling, founder wealth management, equity structuring, and exit planning. You need both, but the advisor drives the long-term wealth strategy.

**How does startup exit planning work?**
Startup exit planning involves structuring the company and the founder's personal finances years in advance of an acquisition or IPO. It includes optimizing tax liabilities, setting up trusts, managing secondary share sales, and ensuring that the financial mechanics of the deal maximize the net return for the founders and early employees.

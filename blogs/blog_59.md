## FAQ

**Why are there so many trading companies on sourcing platforms?**
Trading companies proliferate on these platforms because they are highly skilled at marketing and digital presentation, often investing heavily in premium directory memberships and professional photography. They act as intermediaries, matching buyers with cheap factories, but they rarely disclose their middleman status. This setup allows them to capture a margin without holding any hard assets, which ultimately drives up costs and severely limits quality control for the end buyer.

**How can I tell if a supplier is a real factory or a broker?**
You can expose a broker by demanding highly specific technical details immediately. Ask for the exact models of their injection molding machines, their specific defect rate percentages, and unedited video footage of the factory floor. Real factories can provide this data instantly because they own the equipment; brokers will typically stall, offer vague answers, or try to pivot the conversation back to pricing and sample availability.

**Should I send payments through the directory's platform?**
While some platforms offer escrow services that provide a baseline level of security for small initial sample runs, serious enterprise procurement teams typically move large-scale transactions off the platform. For mass production runs, buyers use established international banking protocols, Letters of Credit, and legally binding commercial contracts to ensure greater financial security, stricter legal recourse, and complete control over the financial transaction.

## Navigating the Mechanics of Corporate PPA Agreements

Corporate PPA agreements work as long-term financial contracts where a corporation agrees to purchase a massive volume of renewable energy from a developer at a strictly negotiated fixed "strike price." In a direct PPA, the physical electricity is delivered to the corporate facility. ## FAQ

**What is the critical difference between a Physical PPA and a Virtual PPA (vPPA)?**
In a Physical PPA, the massive renewable energy project is generally located on the same grid network as the corporate facility, and the physical electrons are actually delivered to the buyer to power their operations. A Virtual PPA (vPPA) is a purely financial derivative (a contract for differences). The massive energy project can be located anywhere; the power is sold into the local wholesale market, and the corporation and developer strictly settle the financial difference between the floating market price and the fixed strike price. The corporation retains the RECs for sustainability claims.

**What is "basis risk" in corporate clean energy contracts?**
Basis risk is the severe financial risk that arises when the wholesale market pricing node where the renewable project sells its energy differs significantly from the pricing node where the corporation physically consumes its energy. If the project's local grid becomes massively congested, the floating market price can collapse, forcing the corporation to pay a massive settlement difference to the developer while still paying high retail utility rates at their physical facilities. Specialized advisors rigorously model this risk before signing.

**Why do developers require an investment-grade corporate off-taker for a PPA?**
Massive utility-scale renewable projects require hundreds of millions of dollars in highly structured project finance. Institutional debt providers will only release this massive capital if the project's long-term revenue is absolutely guaranteed. By signing a 15-to-20-year PPA, a highly stable, investment-grade corporation effectively guarantees this revenue stream. The lender relies entirely on the corporation's massive credit rating, not the developer's balance sheet, to underwrite the massive infrastructure loan.

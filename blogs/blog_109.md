## FAQ

**What do ESG investment advisors do?**
ESG investment advisors help corporations and institutional investors allocate capital based on Environmental, Social, and Governance criteria. They analyze climate risks, assess corporate board diversity, evaluate supply chain ethics, and construct portfolios that generate strong financial returns while adhering to strict sustainability mandates.

**What is the difference between ESG and socially responsible investing (SRI)?**
While related, SRI typically focuses on negative screening—excluding specific industries like tobacco or weapons based on ethical values. ESG is a more comprehensive financial framework that integrates environmental, social, and governance factors into traditional financial analysis to identify material risks and growth opportunities.

**How do you measure the success of a corporate ESG strategy?**
Success is measured through a dual mandate: financial performance benchmarked against standard market indices, and impact performance tracked through specific metrics like portfolio carbon intensity, board diversity ratios, and adherence to international reporting frameworks like the TCFD.

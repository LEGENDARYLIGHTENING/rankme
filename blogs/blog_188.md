## FAQ

**What is the difference between used and refurbished industrial equipment?**
Used industrial equipment is typically sold "as-is" with no guarantees regarding its mechanical condition or future reliability; it simply means the machine was previously owned. Refurbished industrial equipment, however, has been thoroughly inspected, repaired, and tested by certified technicians. Worn parts are replaced, software is often updated, and the machine is restored to a condition that meets the original manufacturer's strict operational specifications.

**Why is an oil analysis important when buying used heavy machinery?**
An oil analysis is a critical diagnostic tool because it reveals the hidden internal health of the machine's engine, hydraulics, and transmission. By analyzing the oil for microscopic metal shavings, coolant leaks, and dirt contamination, technicians can accurately predict impending catastrophic failures that are impossible to detect through a visual inspection alone. It provides the buyer with hard data on how aggressively the previous owner operated and maintained the asset.

**Do environmental regulations apply differently to used industrial equipment?**
Environmental regulations apply strictly to all operating machinery, regardless of its age. However, older used equipment may not meet the current stringent emissions standards, such as Tier 4 Final, required for many modern commercial sites or urban zones. Buyers must verify the local regulatory requirements before purchasing, as outfitting older machinery with compliant aftermarket exhaust scrubbers can be incredibly expensive and sometimes technically impossible.

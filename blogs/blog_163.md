## FAQ

**How long does it typically take to get Halal certified?**
The timeline varies significantly depending on the complexity of your product and the readiness of your facility. For brands with simple ingredient profiles and highly organized supply chain documentation, the process can be completed in three to six months. However, if a facility requires major operational changes, ingredient reformulations, or handles high-risk cross-contamination scenarios, securing full approval can easily take up to a year.

**Do I need a separate certificate for every country I export to?**
Not always, but it is a critical factor to verify. Many countries have mutual recognition agreements where they accept certificates issued by specific, highly reputable global authorities. However, strict markets like Saudi Arabia or the UAE often require accreditation from bodies explicitly registered and approved by their respective national governments. It is essential to confirm destination requirements before applying.

**Can a facility process both compliant and non-compliant foods?**
Yes, but it requires exceptionally strict operational controls. Facilities must implement rigorous segregation protocols, often utilizing dedicated production lines, separate storage areas, and specialized cleaning procedures to guarantee zero cross-contamination. Many strict certifying bodies mandate that thorough, documented sanitization processes, sometimes supervised by an inspector, occur between production runs of different product categories.

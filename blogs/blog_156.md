## FAQ

**What is the critical difference between carbon neutral and net zero?**
Carbon neutrality often relies heavily on purchasing massive amounts of carbon offsets to "cancel out" ongoing emissions without fundamentally changing internal operations. Net zero is a vastly more rigorous standard. Achieving true net zero requires an organization to aggressively reduce its absolute emissions across its entire value chain (usually by 90% or more) in alignment with climate science. Only the absolute minimum, unavoidable residual emissions can be neutralized using highly vetted, permanent carbon removal technologies.

**Why are Scope 3 emissions the most difficult to measure and reduce?**
Scope 3 emissions encompass all indirect emissions occurring within a company's massive value chain, including purchased goods, global logistics, and product end-of-life. They are incredibly difficult to measure because the corporation does not directly control these external entities. Elite consultants deploy advanced supply chain auditing software and mandate strict data disclosures from thousands of global vendors, allowing the corporation to accurately baseline these massive emissions and force aggressive reductions across the entire supplier network.

**How do ESG consultants evaluate the legitimacy of carbon offsets?**
The voluntary carbon market is severely plagued by low-quality, highly questionable credits. Elite consultants protect their corporate clients from massive greenwashing scandals by rigorously vetting every offset purchase. They strictly mandate that credits are verified by top-tier international registries (like Verra or Gold Standard) and demand absolute proof of "additionality"—ensuring the carbon reduction would not have occurred without the offset funding. They also prioritize permanent carbon removal technologies over highly volatile avoidance credits.

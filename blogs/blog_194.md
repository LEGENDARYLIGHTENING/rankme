## FAQ Section

**What is the main difference between personal life insurance and key person insurance?**
The main difference is the policy owner and the beneficiary. In personal life insurance, the executive owns the policy, and their family receives the payout. In key person insurance, the company owns the policy, pays the premiums, and receives the entire payout. This structure is specifically designed to inject massive, immediate liquidity directly into the business to stabilize operations, pay off demanding creditors, and fund the recruitment of a high-level replacement.

**How do you accurately calculate the coverage amount for CEO life insurance?**
Accurately calculating the coverage amount for CEO life insurance requires a deep dive into the company's financials. You must evaluate the executive's direct contribution to overall revenue, the estimated cost and time required to recruit and train a suitable replacement, and any outstanding bank loans that might instantly become due upon their death. A common rule of thumb is to secure coverage equal to five to ten times the executive's total annual compensation, adjusted for specific corporate debt obligations.

**Can executive life insurance policies be used to fund a buy-sell agreement?**
Yes, executive life insurance policies are the absolute best, most reliable mechanism to fund a corporate buy-sell agreement. If a partner or major shareholder suddenly dies, the surviving partners frequently lack the liquid cash required to buy out the deceased's shares from their grieving family. The insurance payout provides immediate, tax-efficient capital to execute the buyout, preventing hostile interference in the business and ensuring a smooth, conflict-free transition of corporate ownership.

## FAQ

**What certifications should a B2B machinery distributor hold?**
A reputable B2B machinery distributor must hold official, verifiable authorization certificates from the original equipment manufacturers they represent. They should also possess relevant ISO certifications, particularly ISO 9001 for quality management systems, to ensure their internal processes meet strict global standards. Depending on the region, they may also need specific import/export compliance credentials and authorized economic operator status.

**How do global machinery sales handle international warranties?**
International warranties are typically managed through a localized service agreement structured by the primary distributor. A reliable distributor will act as the direct liaison between the buyer and the manufacturer, ensuring that local engineering teams are fully authorized to perform warranty repairs without voiding the coverage. You must ensure the contract explicitly states that warranty service will be provided locally, rather than requiring the machinery to be shipped back.

**Why is post-sale integration support so important in factory machinery?**
Modern factory machinery is highly complex and rarely functions as a simple plug-and-play asset. Post-sale integration support is critical because the equipment must be properly calibrated and seamlessly connected to existing programmable logic controllers and factory execution systems. Without expert integration provided by the distributor, the machinery will fail to reach its stated production efficiency and could cause severe safety hazards on the assembly line.

## FAQ

**What is an organic equivalence arrangement?**
An organic equivalence arrangement is a bilateral agreement between two countries (or regions) recognizing each other's organic certification systems as comparable. For example, under the US-EU equivalence arrangement, a product certified as USDA Organic can generally be sold as organic in the European Union without needing to undergo a separate, costly EU certification process. These arrangements drastically reduce the cost and friction of exporting organic foods, though specific labeling rules still apply.

**Can a product lose its organic status during international transit?**
Yes, absolutely. If an organic product is shipped in a container that previously held prohibited chemicals without undergoing a certified cleaning process, or if it is fumigated at customs using banned synthetic pesticides, it immediately loses its organic integrity. Exporters must work strictly with specialized freight forwarders who implement rigorous segregation and handling protocols to ensure the product remains uncontaminated throughout the entire supply chain.

**How do foreign distributors verify organic claims?**
Foreign distributors are highly risk-averse and do not take organic claims at face value. They verify claims by requesting the brand's current organic transaction certificates, which trace the specific batch of products back to the certified manufacturing facility and farms. Furthermore, distributors actively cross-reference the brand's certification numbers with the official online databases maintained by the issuing accrediting bodies to ensure the certificates have not expired or been revoked.

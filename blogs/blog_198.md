## FAQ Section

**Why is relying on a fully-insured health plan often a massive financial mistake for growing companies?**
Relying on a standard fully-insured plan is often a massive financial mistake because the company pays a fixed, extremely high premium regardless of how little healthcare the employees actually use. If your staff is generally healthy and files very few claims, the insurance carrier keeps 100% of the massive profit margin. Moving to alternative structures like level-funding or self-insurance allows the company to retain those massive unspent funds at the end of the year, instantly returning crucial capital directly to the corporate balance sheet.

**What is a pharmacy benefit audit, and why is it absolutely critical for cost control?**
A pharmacy benefit audit is a highly aggressive, forensic review of the complex contracts between your company and your Pharmacy Benefit Manager (PBM). PBMs are notorious for utilizing highly opaque pricing, hidden rebates, and spread pricing to maximize their own massive profits at the employer's direct expense. A specialized broker will conduct a rigorous audit to expose these hidden fees, renegotiate the contract terms, and ensure that all lucrative drug rebates are returned directly to the employer, often instantly saving the company millions.

**How do corporate benefits consultants utilize claims data to design better health plans?**
Corporate benefits consultants utilize detailed, anonymized claims data to identify exactly which specific medical conditions are driving the vast majority of the company's healthcare costs. If the data reveals a massive spike in expensive emergency room visits or unchecked chronic conditions like diabetes, the consultant will aggressively redesign the plan to eliminate those issues. This includes offering completely free primary care visits, mandating specialized chronic disease management programs, and steering employees toward high-quality, lower-cost medical facilities, drastically reducing the frequency of catastrophic claims.

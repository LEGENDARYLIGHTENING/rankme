## FAQ

**What is the difference between a wealth manager and an investment advisory firm?**
While the terms are often used interchangeably, an elite investment advisory firm typically focuses on institutional-grade portfolio management, alternative asset allocation, and serving ultra-high-net-worth individuals or family offices. They operate with a fiduciary duty, often independently, providing unconflicted advice rather than selling proprietary financial products.

**Why do high-net-worth individuals prefer independent investment advisors?**
Independent investment advisors are not tied to a specific bank or wirehouse, meaning they are not incentivized to sell proprietary funds. This open-architecture approach ensures that the advisor can source the absolute best investment opportunities globally, aligning their strategy entirely with the client's best interests.

**What is a family office investment advisory service?**
A family office investment advisory service acts as an outsourced investment committee for a wealthy family. They provide comprehensive oversight of the family's assets, handle due diligence for private market deals, manage risk, and coordinate with tax and legal professionals to ensure smooth multi-generational wealth transfer.

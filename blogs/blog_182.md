## FAQ

**When does it make sense to buy industrial equipment instead of leasing?**
Buying makes the most sense when the equipment has a very long, stable usable life and a slow rate of technological obsolescence. If you have excess cash reserves and can fully utilize the asset's depreciation for tax benefits, purchasing outright can result in a lower total cost of ownership over a ten-year period. It is also preferable for highly customized machinery that cannot be easily returned or repurposed by a leasing company.

**Are lease payments for industrial machinery tax deductible?**
In most jurisdictions, payments made under an operating lease are fully tax deductible as standard business operating expenses. This can significantly reduce the net cost of acquiring the equipment. However, capital leases are treated differently, often allowing you to claim depreciation and interest expenses instead. Always consult with a certified corporate tax advisor to confirm the exact implications for your specific financial situation.

**What happens at the end of an industrial equipment lease?**
At the end of a typical lease term, you generally have three primary options. You can return the equipment to the lessor with no further obligations, renew the lease for an extended period at a potentially reduced rate, or purchase the equipment outright. The purchase price is usually determined by either a pre-agreed residual value or the current fair market value, depending on the specific structure of your initial contract.

## Building a Proactive Compliance Infrastructure

Regulatory compliance is not a one-time hurdle; it is an ongoing operational requirement. Relying on an EFSA compliance consultant or FDA expert solely during product launch leaves you vulnerable to routine audits and sudden regulatory updates.

To build a robust compliance infrastructure, implement these actionable takeaways:
- Conduct biannual audits of your marketing materials, website copy, and social media posts to ensure no unauthorized disease claims have been published.
- Maintain a centralized, secure database of all supplier Certificates of Analysis (COAs) and third-party lab testing results.
- Train your internal marketing team on the strict differences between permissible structure/function claims and prohibited disease treatment claims.
- Establish a clear standard operating procedure (SOP) for handling and documenting adverse event reports from customers.
- Consult with your regulatory partners before reformulating any existing product or changing raw material suppliers.

## FAQ

**What is the difference between FDA approval and FDA compliance for supplements?**
The FDA does not 'approve' dietary supplements before they go to market in the way they approve pharmaceutical drugs. Instead, supplement companies must ensure their products are FDA compliant, meaning they adhere to current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP), use acceptable ingredients, and make only permissible structure/function claims without suggesting the product cures disease.

**Why do I need a consultant just for my supplement labels?**
Supplement labeling laws are incredibly dense, governing everything from the exact font size of the net weight declaration to the specific phrasing of the supplement facts panel. A consultant ensures you do not inadvertently use banned terminology, misclassify ingredients, or make unauthorized health claims that could trigger expensive product recalls.

**Can a regulatory firm help me sell my US supplement in Europe?**
Yes, international regulatory firms specialize in bridging the gap between US and EU standards. They will audit your formula against the strict EFSA regulations, identify any required formulation changes, and manage the complex documentation process needed to legally import and distribute your products across European markets.

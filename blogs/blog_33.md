## What to Actually Look for in a Marketing Agency

**What should I look for in a marketing agency for my aesthetic clinic?**

The answer is specific: look for an agency or consultant that can demonstrate direct experience with medical aesthetics marketing, evidence of actual patient acquisition outcomes (not just traffic or follower growth), understanding of the regulatory constraints in your market (ASA in the UK, DHA in UAE, TGA in Australia), and a technical capability that extends beyond social media management. Any agency pitching you on Instagram growth as the primary metric is not the right fit. Social media has a role in aesthetics marketing — particularly for trust-building and retargeting — but it is a supporting channel, not a primary acquisition channel for most clinics.

Ask prospective agencies these questions: What percentage of your clinic clients' new patients come from organic search versus paid ads? Can you show me examples of clinic websites you've built and their Core Web Vitals scores? Do you understand the advertising restrictions for Botox and prescription-only medicines in my market? How do you approach GEO optimisation for AI search tools? If an agency can't answer all four questions confidently, keep looking.

Clinic patient acquisition in 2026 is not a single-channel problem. The clinics generating a predictable flow of high-value patients use a full-stack approach where each channel handles a different stage of the patient journey:

- **Email and WhatsApp follow-up**: The most underrated retention tool. Clinics that have a structured follow-up sequence for enquiries who don't immediately book recover a significant percentage of those leads.

An aesthetic clinic marketing agency worth retaining coordinates all of these channels toward the same patient acquisition goals — not just runs whichever channels they're most comfortable billing for.

## FAQ

**How much should a medical aesthetics clinic spend on marketing?**
Most established aesthetic clinics allocate between five and fifteen percent of their monthly revenue to marketing. For a clinic generating £50,000 per month, that's £2,500 to £7,500 — split across SEO, paid ads, content, and website maintenance. Early-stage clinics may need to invest a higher percentage to build initial visibility. The key metric isn't what you spend — it's your cost per acquisition. A well-run Google Ads campaign for aesthetic treatments typically delivers a cost per booked patient between £30 and £120, depending on treatment type and location.

**Can an aesthetic clinic market Botox on Instagram?**
Yes, with restrictions. Meta prohibits the promotion of prescription medicines directly, and the UK ASA restricts before/after imagery in paid ads. Organic Instagram content showing clinic culture, practitioner expertise, and education about treatments is generally compliant. Paid promotion of aesthetic treatments must follow platform-specific rules and local regulations. Working with an agency that understands these rules prevents costly ad suspensions and ASA complaints.

**What's the difference between SEO and GEO for an aesthetic clinic?**
SEO (Search Engine Optimization) focuses on ranking your website in traditional Google search results. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) focuses on structuring your content so AI tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity reference your clinic in generated answers. Both are important in 2026 — SEO drives traffic from patients who click through results, while GEO builds visibility with patients who ask AI tools for clinic recommendations without clicking any link. A complete digital strategy for aesthetic clinics includes both.

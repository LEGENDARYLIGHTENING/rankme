## FAQ

**What is the difference between preventative and predictive maintenance contracts?**
Preventative maintenance contracts involve scheduling routine service based strictly on a calendar or set machine hours, much like changing a car's oil every 5,000 miles. Predictive maintenance contracts are far more advanced; they utilize live sensor data and telematics to continuously monitor the machine's exact condition. The service provider intervenes only when the data indicates a component is actively beginning to fail, maximizing the part's lifespan and virtually eliminating unnecessary downtime.

**Are parts always included in a machinery service agreement?**
Parts are not always automatically included in a standard machinery service agreement. Basic agreements often cover only the routine labor and diagnostic checks, leaving the buyer responsible for purchasing the replacement components. Comprehensive or "full-maintenance" contracts typically include the cost of all standard wear parts and major components, providing a highly predictable, fixed operational cost, though these premium contracts carry a significantly higher monthly premium.

**How do emergency response guarantees work in B2B equipment service?**
Emergency response guarantees are strict contractual obligations dictating how quickly a certified technician must be onsite following a reported breakdown. These guarantees vary based on the severity of the failure and the location of the machinery, often ranging from 4 to 24 hours. A strong contract will include specific financial penalties or service credits applied to the buyer's account if the provider fails to meet these critical response metrics.

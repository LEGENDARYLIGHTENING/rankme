# Technical Requirements for Strong GEO Performance

**By Moksh, Founder of Rankur**

The digital marketing industry is currently flooded with generic advice on Generative Engine Optimization (GEO). Agencies tell B2B companies to "write better content" or "mention your brand more." 

This advice completely misses the point. GEO is fundamentally an engineering problem, not a copywriting problem. 

When an AI engine like Perplexity or ChatGPT scrapes the web to answer a complex enterprise procurement query, it evaluates the structural integrity of your code before it ever processes your words. If your infrastructure is bloated, slow, or unstructured, the AI will abandon the crawl and cite a technically superior competitor. 

To dominate the generative landscape, you must implement a rigorous **B2B GEO technical setup**. This is the definitive **B2B generative technical checklist** for enterprise infrastructure.

## Requirement 1: Server-Side Rendering (SSR) or Static Site Generation (SSG)

AI scraping bots (like standard Googlebots) struggle immensely with Client-Side Rendering (CSR). 

If you build your B2B website as a standard Single Page Application (SPA) where JavaScript must execute in the browser before the content (like pricing or compliance data) becomes visible, the AI bot will likely scrape a blank page. It does not have the time or resources to render complex JS to find your data.

**The Fix:**
You must migrate your architecture to a headless framework like Next.js that utilizes Server-Side Rendering (SSR) or Static Site Generation (SSG). This ensures that the moment the AI bot hits your URL, the entire factual payload of the HTML is immediately available in the source code. This is non-negotiable for **requirements for B2B GEO**.

## Requirement 2: Comprehensive JSON-LD Schema

We cannot overstate the importance of Schema Markup in GEO. AI models rely on structured data to categorize entities, verify facts, and mitigate hallucination. 

If your website relies on standard HTML paragraphs to explain your services, you are failing. You must deploy aggressive JSON-LD across your architecture. 

**The Essential GEO Schemas:**
1. **Organization Schema:** Validates your corporate entity, HQ location, and social links.
2. **Person Schema:** Validates the human authority of your content authors (E-E-A-T).
3. **Service / Product Schema:** Mathematically defines your exact capabilities, pricing, and compliance standards. 
4. **FAQPage Schema:** Spoon-feeds the AI exact answers to common buyer prompts. 

This code must be dynamically generated and perfectly validated. A single syntax error will break the entire graph. 

## Requirement 3: Semantic HTML Hierarchy

AI models use semantic HTML to understand the contextual weight of the information on your page. 

If your developers are using generic `<div>` tags and styling them to look like headings, the AI has no way of knowing what the core topic is. 

**The Fix:**
You must enforce a rigid HTML hierarchy. 
- You may only have one `<h1>` tag per page, representing the absolute core topic.
- Subtopics must be sequentially nested within `<h2>` and `<h3>` tags. 
- When providing lists or technical specifications, you must use proper `<ul>`, `<ol>`, or semantic HTML `<table>` tags. AI synthesis engines prioritize data found in properly coded tables when generating comparative overviews.

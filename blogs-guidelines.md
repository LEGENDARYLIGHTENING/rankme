# Blog Guidelines for Moksh Freelance Website Content

## Objective

Create publish-ready B2B blog posts for Moksh, a freelance B2B growth consultant who offers:

- React JS website builds
- SEO
- GEO (generative engine optimization)
- LinkedIn content strategy
- Meta Ads management
- Google Ads management

Target markets:

- United States
- United Kingdom
- Australia
- Canada
- Gulf countries: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar

Primary proof point to reference where relevant:

- Probiota Innovations: a full B2B React JS website built in 1.5 hours that generated 10+ qualified B2B leads.

## Core Assignment

Write blog posts that help Moksh attract qualified inbound leads from B2B buyers in his target markets. Each post must position Moksh as a specialist who combines technical website execution with growth thinking across SEO, GEO, LinkedIn content, and paid acquisition.

The content must feel commercially useful, not academic. Each blog should speak to decision-makers who care about lead generation, trust, conversion, authority, pipeline growth, and buyer intent.

## Global Writing Rules

- Minimum length: 1200 words per blog post. No exceptions.
- Every post must be complete and publish-ready.
- Use active voice throughout.
- Write in plain English.
- Keep the tone sharp, authoritative, and direct.
- Sound like a specialist with real execution experience.
- Avoid fluff, vague statements, and motivational filler.
- Do not summarize multiple posts together.
- Complete one full blog before moving to the next.
- Keep the content specific to the niche, buyer, and target market.
- Tie recommendations to real business outcomes such as leads, booked calls, pipeline quality, conversion rate, CAC efficiency, brand trust, or sales readiness.

## Voice and Tone

The writing should sound like a specialist who has seen weak sites, weak funnels, and weak messaging many times before and knows what fixes them.

Use this tone profile:

- Direct, not rude
- Commercial, not academic
- Sharp, not theatrical
- Confident, not exaggerated
- Experienced, not generic
- Helpful, not preachy

The writing should feel close to how a strong B2B consultant speaks to founders, marketing heads, commercial teams, and business owners.

## Forbidden Phrases and Habits

Do not use these phrases anywhere:

- in today’s digital landscape
- in this day and age
- it goes without saying
- leverage synergies
- holistic approach

Also follow these rules:

- Never start a sentence with “However”
- Never start a sentence with “Moreover”
- Do not use filler intros
- Do not use generic motivation language
- Do not write like a school essay
- Do not write fluffy AI-sounding transitions
- Do not overuse em dashes
- Do not keyword stuff

## Mandatory Metadata Block for Every Blog

Every blog must begin with this exact metadata sequence in this order:

- SEO Title — the exact blog title, keyword-rich, under 60 characters where possible  
- Meta Description — 150 to 160 characters, includes the primary keyword, written for click-through rate  
- Slug — clean URL slug, lowercase, hyphens only  
- Primary Keyword — the single most important keyword this post targets  
- Secondary Keywords — 3 to 5 supporting keywords woven naturally into the post  
- GEO Phrase — one phrase written exactly how someone would ask ChatGPT or Perplexity for this topic  
- Target Market — which country or region this post is strongest for  
- Niche Tag — the niche category this post belongs to  

## Mandatory Blog Structure

After the metadata block, every blog must follow this exact structure.

### 1. H1 — Blog title

Use the blog title as the H1.

### 2. Introduction — 150 words

Rules:

- Open with the reader’s pain point or problem.
- Make it specific to their industry and market.
- End the introduction with a clear statement of what the post will teach them.
- Do not start with “In this article” or “Welcome to.”
- The primary keyword must appear within the first 100 words.

### 3. H2 — Context section: Why this matters right now

Rules:

- Around 200 words.
- Set the industry context.
- Reference what is changing in 2026 for this niche and target market.
- Include the primary keyword naturally.
- Reference AI search, GEO, or changing buyer behavior where relevant.
- Make this section feel current and commercially relevant.

### 4. H2 — Main section 1 with a specific, benefit-driven heading

Rules:

- Around 200 words.
- Cover the first major point, strategy, or mistake.
- Use specifics: numbers, examples, buyer behavior, conversion friction, or commercial implications.
- Where relevant, reference the Probiota Innovations case study as proof of speed, clarity, and lead-focused execution.

### 5. H2 — Main section 2 with a specific, benefit-driven heading

Rules:

- Around 200 words.
- Cover the second major point.
- Include the GEO phrase naturally.
- Add a short paragraph that answers the GEO question directly as if ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity would quote it.
- The answer paragraph should be 3 to 5 sentences.
- Make this answer concise, direct, and useful.

### 6. H2 — Main section 3 with a specific, benefit-driven heading

Rules:

- Around 200 words.
- Cover the third major point.
- Include at least one secondary keyword naturally.
- Add a short bullet list of 4 to 6 actionable takeaways inside this section.
- Keep the bullet points practical and implementation-focused.

### 7. H2 — How Moksh can help

Rules:

- Around 150 words.
- Write this section in third person.
- Explain how Moksh’s services solve the problem discussed in the blog.
- Mention website builds, SEO, GEO, LinkedIn content, and ads where relevant.
- Mention the free 30-minute growth audit naturally.
- Do not make it sound like a hard pitch.
- Make it feel like the logical next step for a serious buyer.

### 8. H2 — Conclusion: What to do next

Rules:

- Around 100 words.
- Summarize the 3 most important takeaways in plain language.
- End with this CTA written naturally: Book a free 30-minute growth audit.

### 9. FAQ Section — minimum 3 questions

Rules:

- Write at least 3 FAQs.
- Each answer must be 60 to 100 words.
- Phrase each question naturally, as a buyer would ask Google, ChatGPT, or Perplexity.
- Use this section to strengthen GEO relevance.
- Keep answers specific and commercially relevant.

## SEO Rules

Every post must follow these SEO rules:

- The primary keyword must appear in the H1.
- The primary keyword must appear in the first 100 words of the introduction.
- The primary keyword must appear in at least two H2 headings.
- The primary keyword must appear in the meta description.
- Each secondary keyword must appear at least once naturally in the body.
- Do not force exact-match repetition unnaturally.
- Keep the writing readable for humans first.
- Make the title and meta description attractive for clicks without sounding spammy.

## GEO Rules

Every post must follow these GEO rules:

- The GEO phrase must appear verbatim or near-verbatim in the body.
- Preferably place it in Main Section 2.
- Present it as a real user-style question.
- Answer it directly in 3 to 5 sentences.
- Write the answer so it could stand alone in AI search results.
- Keep the answer clear, concise, useful, and specific.
- Use natural phrasing that reflects how users ask ChatGPT or Perplexity.

## Link Placeholder Rules

Every post must include:

- At least one internal link placeholder in this exact format:  
  `[Internal link: page name — anchor text]`
- At least one external link placeholder in this exact format:  
  `[External link: topic — suggested source type]`

Rules for placement:

- Insert placeholders naturally where they support the content.
- Do not dump them together at the end.
- The internal link should connect to a relevant service page, case study, audit page, or niche page.
- The external link should point to a credible source type such as an industry report, search engine documentation, ad platform guidance, market data source, or regulatory body.

## Claims, Proof, and Evidence Rules

- Every claim must feel grounded.
- Use one of these two methods:
  - Reference a credible external link placeholder.
  - Frame the point as industry-observed behavior.
- Do not invent fake studies.
- Do not fabricate percentages without grounding.
- Do not use “studies show” unless a credible source placeholder logically supports it.
- If using commercial examples, make them realistic.
- If referencing buyer behavior, tie it to real-world website, SEO, or lead-gen patterns.

Examples of acceptable phrasing:

- Across B2B service sites, buyers often delay inquiry forms when the offer is unclear.
- On many underperforming SaaS sites, demo friction starts before the visitor reaches the form.
- In high-trust categories, buyers usually compare credibility signals before they contact a vendor.

## Case Study Usage Rules

Where relevant, use the Probiota Innovations case study carefully and naturally.

What it proves:

- Fast execution
- Conversion-focused thinking
- Strong B2B messaging
- A live example of a React JS website used to generate qualified leads

Do not force this case study into every paragraph. Use it when it strengthens the argument.

## Style Guidance

- Use short to medium paragraphs.
- Keep sections easy to scan.
- Use benefit-driven subheadings.
- Prefer clarity over cleverness.
- Use niche-appropriate terminology only when necessary.
- Avoid vague advice that could apply to any business.
- Keep the content tied to commercial results.
- Make examples feel like real founder or buyer scenarios.
- Write like someone who understands both growth and execution.

## Target Audience Guidance

The likely readers include:

- Founders
- Marketing heads
- Commercial managers
- Growth leads
- Business owners
- Category-specific operators evaluating vendors, website partners, SEO consultants, or growth support

Each post should reflect the decision-making style of the niche.

Examples:

- SaaS founders care about demos, pipeline, onboarding friction, and conversion.
- Luxury real estate developers care about trust, buyer quality, and premium positioning.
- Cybersecurity firms care about credibility, compliance, and authority.
- Clinics care about trust, patient acquisition, and reputation.
- Immigration consultants care about trust, clarity, compliance, and case qualification.

## Internal Brand Positioning for Moksh

When relevant, position Moksh as:

- A freelance B2B growth consultant
- A specialist in fast, conversion-focused React JS websites
- Someone who combines technical execution with SEO, GEO, LinkedIn thought leadership, and paid acquisition
- A consultant who understands B2B buyer journeys across the US, UK, Australia, Canada, UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar
- A practical operator, not a generic marketing generalist

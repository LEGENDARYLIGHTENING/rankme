## FAQ

**What is Vendor Managed Inventory (VMI) in industrial procurement?**
Vendor Managed Inventory (VMI) is a supply chain agreement where the supplier takes full responsibility for maintaining an agreed-upon inventory of specific spare parts physically located at the buyer's facility. The supplier monitors the stock levels, often through connected digital systems, and automatically replenishes the components as they are used. This drastically reduces the buyer's administrative procurement burden and guarantees immediate parts availability for critical maintenance tasks.

**How does predictive maintenance affect spare parts ordering?**
Predictive maintenance transforms spare parts ordering from a reactive scramble into a highly planned, automated process. By using sensors to monitor machine health, software can predict exactly when a component is likely to fail. This allows procurement systems to automatically order the necessary part weeks in advance, using standard, cost-effective shipping methods rather than expensive emergency overnight freight, effectively eliminating unplanned downtime.

**Why is standardizing machinery important for parts procurement?**
Standardizing machinery across a facility is crucial because it drastically reduces the complexity and sheer volume of the spare parts inventory required. When a factory uses motors, drives, and hydraulic valves from a single manufacturer across multiple assembly lines, the maintenance team only needs to stock a few variations of each component. This lowers capital tied up in warehouse stock and simplifies the training required for the maintenance technicians.
